Chapter 1

The Awakening

She woke to find the world had ended—quietly, without announcement, the way dawn replaces night without ever asking permission. Lyra Voss lay still in the bed she had always known, in the room she had always occupied, and understood with absolute certainty that nothing around her was real anymore.

The ceiling was the same pale blue it had always been. The curtains moved in the same familiar breeze. Her mother's voice drifted up the stairs, calling her name in the same gentle cadence it always had. But underneath all of that sameness, Lyra could hear the fractures—thin, crystalline sounds, like the cracking of ice in spring, spreading through the fabric of every ordinary thing.

She had dreamed of the library again. Not the small, comfortable one on Vander Street where she had spent her childhood afternoons, but the other one—the impossible one, built from the spines of books that hadn't been written yet, its corridors stretching into dark infinities she could never quite reach. In the dream she had found a door. Behind the door was a mirror. In the mirror was a woman who was not her, wearing her face.

"The second self," the woman had said, and smiled, and reached through the glass.

Lyra sat up. Her hands were shaking. On her palm, where no mark had existed when she fell asleep, was a symbol she had seen once before—carved into the cornerstone of the old temple at the edge of town, the one that the locals said was older than language itself.

She pressed her thumb against it. The fractures in the world grew louder.

Somewhere, in a time that hadn't happened yet, a door was waiting to be opened.
