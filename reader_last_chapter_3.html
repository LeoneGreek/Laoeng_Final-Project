Chapter 3

Gathering Storms

The fractures were visible now. Not just audible—visible. Thin lines of luminous darkness spreading across the sky above the town like cracks in old porcelain, each one pulsing faintly with the rhythm of something vast and patient that had been waiting for exactly this moment.

Lyra stood in the center of the town square and watched people go about their lives. None of them could see the fractures. None of them heard the crystalline sounds she had been living with since the morning of her awakening. She had tried, once, to describe what she was seeing to her friend Cass, and the blank incomprehension on Cass's face had told her everything she needed to know about how alone she was in this.

The Oracle had said: find the Door of Unmaking before the next fracture widens.

Lyra had found three more fractures since then. Whatever "next" meant, it had passed. The timeline was running out of thread.

She pressed her thumb to the mark on her palm. It was warm now, all the time, a low steady heat like an ember that refused to go cold. In her chest, she could feel the second soul the Oracle had mentioned—a presence she had always half-sensed but never acknowledged, like a sound just below the threshold of hearing. Now it was louder. Now it was almost a voice.

"I know," she said, quietly, to no one anyone else could see. "I know. We're going."

She turned east, toward the mountain where the old maps said the Door had been built. Toward the choice that would unmake one of them and save everything else.

The storms gathering on the horizon moved with her, as if they too had been waiting for someone to finally start walking.
