Chapter 2

The Oracle's Warning

The whispers in her mind had changed. Now they spoke a single word, over and over: "Choose." Lyra had followed the mark on her palm to the edge of town, to the old temple with its cornerstone carved in symbols that no scholar had ever deciphered. The doors had opened for her without being touched.

Inside, seated cross-legged on a dais of cracked marble, was the Oracle. She was older than anything Lyra had ever seen—older than the temple, older than the town, possibly older than the hill the town had been built upon. Her eyes were the color of deep water, and they saw too much.

"You have the mark," the Oracle said. Not a question.

"I woke up with it," Lyra said. "I don't know what it means."

"It means you are the one who carries two souls. It means the fractures you have been hearing are real. It means that time, which has been patient for ten thousand years, has finally run out." The Oracle's voice was very calm, the way the sea is calm before a storm. "You have a choice to make. Choose wrongly, and everything that has ever existed will cease to have existed. Choose rightly, and you will cease to exist yourself."

Lyra felt the cold of the temple floor through the soles of her shoes. "Those are the only options?"

"There is always a third option," the Oracle said. "There always is. But no one has ever found it. I mention it only so that you do not feel entirely without hope."

She paused.

"Find the Door of Unmaking before the next fracture widens. What you find behind it will tell you which choice to make." She closed her ancient eyes. "I am sorry I cannot tell you more. The future of this particular thread is the one thing I am not permitted to see."

Lyra left the temple with more questions than she had arrived with, which she suspected was exactly what the Oracle had intended.
